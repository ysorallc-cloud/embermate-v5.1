3 Design Approaches to Reduce Caregiver Overwhelm

The Problem

The current design overwhelms tired caregivers with:

  • Too many similar-looking cards stacked vertically
  • No visual breaks between different types of content
  • Unclear hierarchy - everything looks equally important
  • Requires scrolling and scanning to understand sections
Mockup 1
Minimal Sectioned
"Breathe between sections"
Clean rows with visual dividers. No cards, just breathing room and clear separation.
  • Full-width tinted sections
  • Horizontal dividers
  • Icon + text headers
  • List-style medications
  • Most scannable
Best For
Caregivers who want maximum efficiency

Mockup 2
Hero Card Focus
"One thing at a time"
Giant hero card for next medication. Everything else collapsed. Maximum whitespace and hand-holding.
  • Single focus point
  • Giant CTA button
  • Minimal progress dots
  • "+X more" collapsed
  • 80% breathing room
Best For
Extremely exhausted caregivers
View Mockup 2 →
Mockup 3
Dashboard Panels
"Color-coded zones"
Colored panels for different content. Green = progress, Cream = meds, Blue = appointments.
  • Color-coded sections
  • Full-bleed panels
  • Expand/collapse
  • Dashboard feel
  • Visual organization
Best For
Visual thinkers who like structure
